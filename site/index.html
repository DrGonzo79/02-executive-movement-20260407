Problem

The market is slow where it matters.

New executives often influence budget and vendor decisions in their first 30 to 90 days, but most outbound teams discover those changes only after LinkedIn, ZoomInfo, and CRM sources catch up.

Why the gap exists

  • Source data appears before mainstream systems digest it.
  • Human review creates latency in decision and execution.
  • Most incumbents operate on periodic refresh cycles, not continuous reaction.
  • Speed gaps compound when downstream follow-up is fragmented.

Solution

Build a system that sees it first and moves cleanly.

Monitor press releases, SEC filings, and executive hiring signals in real time, then enrich likely contact pathways and launch hyper-relevant outreach before every other SDR sequence hits their inbox.

Core workflow

Detect appointment news → extract name/title/company → enrich likely email pattern → score by buying relevance → launch personalized outreach.

Relevant data sources

PR Newswire, SEC 8-K filings, company newsroom feeds, Google News, enrichment APIs.

Strategic wedge

Real-time executive change detection creates a timing edge in outbound.

How this turns into a real business
